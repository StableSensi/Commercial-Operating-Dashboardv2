Definitions & method

The standard behind every report, in one page: how reports are written so they stay correct over time, and what every number means. If a figure can't be defined here, it doesn't ship.

Owner: Sales & Marketing · Confidential

The method, in short

This is a point-in-time archive, not a live dashboard. Every item is dated; figures live inside each dated report, never on the home page. A published report is closed at publication — corrections appear as restatements in the next report's appendix, never as edits to a published page.

The two-weeks-later test

Every page must read correctly two weeks after publication without being touched. Dated facts only — never "next week" or "upcoming". Reports record what happened; pending to-dos stay in the CRM. The only forward-looking block is the priorities section: a few month-level commitments with owner and date.

Every monthly report follows the same three-tier spine — executive read (verdict, summary, scorecard), operating detail (pipeline, events, partnerships, findings, priorities), and a collapsed appendix (full deal book, CRM snapshot, sources). A thin month gets one honest line, never filler. Scorecards show value + month-over-month delta only; targets and RAG status switch on once monthly targets are set.

Headline metrics

Opportunities in CRM (total). Every opportunity record on the board across all stages, including Backlog, Strategic long-term and Not interested.

Active (working). The sum of the stages where a deal is actively being worked: Partnerships + New + Screening + First Meeting + Qualification + Onboarding. Excludes Strategic long-term, Backlog and Not interested. Always reconciles: active + inactive = total.

New-stage opps. The count in the New stage at the snapshot date.

Advancing. The subset of active deals where a meeting was held or the stage moved in the period. This is the conversion-quality metric, identified each month from CRM notes.

Partnerships advanced. Strategic partner relationships that moved materially in the period (a concrete offer or commitment made) — not the Partnerships stage count.

Marketing-sourced. An opportunity is attributed to an event or campaign if first contact or re-activation happened through it, recorded in the CRM. Owned by Marketing.

Rules

Delta

Each KPI shows month-over-month change vs the prior month's figure for the same metric. Direction colour: green up, red down, neutral flat. Delta-only — see targets.

Targets / RAG

Off for now. RAG colours and "vs target" switch on only once monthly commercial targets are set. Until then, no RAG dots on KPIs.

Restatement

If a prior month's number is corrected after a CRM cleanup, it is stated in the current report's appendix ("April active restated 20 → 22 after cleanup"). Trend series always use corrected numbers. Published reports are never edited.

"As of" discipline

Every report's scorecard cites the CRM snapshot date the counts were read from. The snapshot image is archived in the report folder.

Honesty

Stalled and out-of-region deals are stated plainly and moved to Backlog / Not interested — never carried in Active as false hope.

Source of record

  • Counts: the monthly CRM opportunities board snapshot (archived as reports/YYYY-MM/crm-snapshot.png).
  • Named deals, movement, the advancing test: the month's CRM notes, event/trip reports and weekly updates.
  • Marketing metrics: supplied by Marketing each month.
