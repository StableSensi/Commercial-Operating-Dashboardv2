Before start — contract and checks

StepWhat happens
Contract signedThe individual employment contract is the foundation of the employment relationship — hours, salary, any performance bonus terms all live there. The handbook supplements it, never replaces it.
Background checkDepending on the role, a fit & proper assessment may apply: criminal record extract, credit information or other relevant documentation.
Equipment preparedWorkstation and, where relevant, a company laptop. The employee carries responsibility for issued hardware from handover; company material never moves to private devices.
Handbook issuedThe current staff handbook is available on the shared drive; the new employee is expected to read it thoroughly before or at start.

Day 1 — standards and orientation

  • Welcome and the three pillars — integrity, compliance, quality: what they mean in daily work, and why they are existential for a regulated business.
  • Working frame — office hours Monday–Friday 09:00–17:00; weekly hours per the individual contract; flexibility expected around deadlines, regulatory reporting, audits and investor meetings.
  • Professional standards — dress code and professional appearance: clean and presentable, discreet on tattoos/piercings/jewellery; no ripped jeans, shorts or sportswear.
  • Primary contact — the nearest leader is the first point of contact for planning, leave, wellbeing, training needs and task questions.
  • Safety orientation — first-aid equipment, fire exits, evacuation routes.

First weeks — compliance and security training

The handbook mandates introduction and ongoing training in the rules that govern the work. The first-weeks block covers:

TrainingCovers
ConfidentialityThe heightened duty of confidentiality: internal affairs, customer and partner information — during and after employment.
Insider rulesInsider trading rules, market-abuse rules, conflicts of interest.
AML / KYCAnti-money-laundering and counter-terror-financing prevention.
IT securityPasswords and two-factor authentication, locking workstations, e-mail and document handling, incident reporting; no company material on private devices; work primarily on the central server, not local drives.
Communication dutyTruthful, complete communication in all calls: never guess — say so and come back with verified information; no personal statements that could bind Aryze without management approval.

Ongoing — the rhythms that continue

  • Spot checks — random samples of work (customer communication, transaction documentation, processes) at most every 30 days, as part of regulatory discipline.
  • Annual safety training — first aid, fire drills and emergency-procedure reviews are mandatory.
  • Professional development — internal programmes and agreed external courses, prioritising compliance/KYC/AML and IT security; the gross-salary scheme is available for further education such as an HD or MBA.
  • Duty to inform — material changes in personal circumstances that could affect fitness for the role are reported to management immediately.
  • Whistleblower route — serious concerns can be raised internally to any member of management or the board, or anonymously via the national scheme.

The role-specific plan

The handbook defines standards and training. The role-specific 30/60/90 plan — goals, buddy, first deliverables — is set per hire by the nearest leader.
