Sales-to-Onboarding process

From commercial commitment to live operation. Clear context, ownership and readiness at every gate.

Owner: Commercial · Aryze commercial flow · 2026 · Confidential

What changesReadiness-led handoffSales does not move a customer forward until product path, KYB status, technical scope, blockers and next milestone are clear.
Why it mattersContext is the deliverableThe receiving team should not rediscover the deal, the use case or the customer's operating constraints.
What it enablesA cleaner startCommercial fit, KYB, IT, legal and owners become visible before the handoff. Not after.

This process begins where the Sales Strategy ends. The boundary is the merchant agreement: when the agreement is sent, the deal is commercially won, and onboarding owns everything after it. The purpose here is to give Sales, Legal, Compliance, Technical and Onboarding the same context at that handoff, so the customer experiences continuity rather than a restart.

Readiness flow

Six stages from commercial commitment to live. Two decision points. One handoff standard. Confirm. Document. Verify. Hand off. Trust work runs in parallel with implementation alignment. Handoff happens when readiness is clear, not when momentum is loud.

Flexible — strict at handoff

Flexible: follow-ups are expected when technical, legal, commercial or stakeholder clarity is needed.

Strict at handoff: onboarding starts only when the packet is complete and the readiness gates have passed.

Readiness gates by product

The packet stays constant. The gates differ.

Pay by Bank — KYB and integration run in parallel

  1. Merchant agreement sent. The commercial win; onboarding starts here.
  2. KYB + technical integration — in parallel, not sequential. Company verification, documentation and approvals run alongside callbacks, reporting, sandbox journeys and theming. Neither waits for the other.
  3. Setup verified. Integration complete and tested end to end.
  4. Go live. KYB approved + integration ready. Monitoring, support, handover to account management.

Stablecoin Factory — three gates before scoping

  • Technical readiness. Customer has a technical team able to use sandbox and docs.
  • Issuer model. Customer (or parent/partner) holds the licence. Aryze does not deliver one.
  • Motion fit. Own Programme / Partner-Led / White-Label confirmed before commercial scoping.

Digital Cash — three institutional gates

  • Licence. Institution already licensed for digital cash or related activity.
  • Executive sponsor. Board-adjacent champion identified before deal progression.
  • Multi-stakeholder capacity. Treasury, legal, compliance, risk and tech can engage in the same room.

A single review meeting is enough when context is clear. Two meetings (commercial + implementation) when KYB, technical or legal questions need a forum. More than two means the deal is not ready for handoff.

Time targets

Internal expectations, not customer promises. A slip past target without a documented reason escalates.

Pay by Bank — 6–12 weeks SMB

  • Commitment → KYB started: 5 working days.
  • KYB started → approved: 1–3 weeks (customer-doc dependent).
  • KYB approved → integration started: 5 working days.
  • Integration → live merchant: 2–6 weeks.

Stablecoin Factory — weeks to a quarter

  • Commitment → sandbox-to-test-net: 2 weeks.
  • Sandbox → first deployment: 2–6 weeks.
  • Own Programme cycle: weeks to a quarter.
  • Partner-Led / White-Label: one to two quarters / two quarters+.

Digital Cash — 6–18 months

  • Commitment → first workshop: 3 weeks.
  • Workshop → architecture: 4–6 weeks.
  • Architecture → legal / risk / procurement: 2–6 months.
  • Phase-1 deployment: 4–6 months on narrow scope.

How we measure handoff quality

The process is judged on whether onboarding starts with context, not whether it starts fast.

Conversion

  • Commitment → readiness review
  • Readiness review → KYB started
  • KYB → onboarding-ready
  • Onboarding-ready → implementation start

Speed

  • Commitment to readiness review
  • Readiness review to KYB start
  • Implementation review to handoff
  • Time stuck with unresolved blockers

Quality

  • Product path documented
  • Stakeholders identified before handoff
  • KYB and legal blockers known
  • Onboarding starts without re-discovery
The decision question

Does each handoff give onboarding enough context, ownership and readiness to move without avoidable rework?
