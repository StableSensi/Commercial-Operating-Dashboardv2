The principle

A go-live timeline is not quoted in a first call. The honest timeline is the sum of four steps measured against the merchant's specific stack, so it is quoted in weeks after step one, not before. No "live in 2 weeks" promises.

The four steps

1 · Map the payment flow

Before any integration work: what is actually moving. Volumes per market, average ticket per cohort, where friction shows up today, and which payment methods sit alongside what we add. The integration timeline is a function of this map. This step runs during the sales dialogue; the merchant agreement marks the handoff into onboarding proper.

2 + 3 · KYB and integration — in parallel

Once the merchant agreement is sent, both tracks start at the same time:

  • KYB and approvals. Know your business (KYB): company verification, documentation, approvals. Optimistic proposals often skip this step, and skipping it slows deals down. Duration depends on jurisdiction, documentation completeness, and how quickly the merchant's team responds to information requests.
  • Integration and test. Integration against the sandbox, with the merchant's engineering team. Sandbox-first evaluation: the conversation after sandbox is contractual; the conversation before sandbox is informational.

4 · Go live

KYB approved + integration tested. Monitoring, support, handover. The team that integrates stays for the first weeks of live volume, because that is when the operational questions surface. Go-live is a phase, not a hand-off event.

Deliberately not in the box

  • PIS-only. No AIS, account verification, balance checks or aggregation.
  • Customer → merchant only. No payouts as a standalone flow.
  • No standalone recurring under PSD2; recurring only via MPS DK/NO until PSD3 (~2027–28).

The next concrete step

For a qualified merchant — average ticket above the cost threshold, real chargeback/VAMP exposure, engineering capacity for a structured integration — the next step is sandbox access. Everything before sandbox is informational; everything after is contractual.
