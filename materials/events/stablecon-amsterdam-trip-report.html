Stablecon EMEA 2026 — Amsterdam

Executive summary

Stablecon EMEA 2026 in Amsterdam met its single objective: identify the right partners and the right customers for Aryze's three product lines. The event produced seven substantive commercial conversations and one invite-only C-level dinner.

Two outcomes stand out. BVNK offered to make their rails available to Aryze. Visa indicated they can help close the payout gap on the Pay by Bank side — a meaningful capability addition, since Pay by Bank is PIS-only (payment initiation services) and customer-to-merchant only, with no standalone payout flow. In both cases the follow-up sat with Aryze at close.

Mastercard, Ripple and Quantoz were at earlier stages, each with a named internal owner. Rabobank and Dune sat further back in the qualification queue. The two most important outcomes of the trip were Visa and BVNK.

Partners — infrastructure & rails

Visa

  • Counterparts: Maike (Visa) and Diana Cabrera (Visa)
  • Status: Open posture toward partnership. Visa indicated they only need us to specify what we want from the relationship.
  • Stated call to action (CTA) from Visa: off-ramp / push function — and specifically, Visa can help with the payout need we have on the Pay by Bank side.
  • Agreed: Aryze brings a partnership presentation — capability map, partnership needs, current pipeline. Owner: Jodi.

Strategic read: this is the single most important commercial signal from the trip. Pay by Bank today is PIS-only and customer-to-merchant only — there is no standalone payout flow under PSD2, and recurring is restricted to MPS DK/NO until PSD3 (~2027–28). Visa Direct push payments would close the payout gap. That would let us put a complete inbound-plus-payout proposition in front of merchants without changing Aryze's infrastructure posture or capability boundaries.

Beyond Pay by Bank, the same off-ramp / push-function angle also fits the recurring on/off-ramp requests from Stablecoin Factory and Digital Cash customers, so the Visa relationship is relevant to all three products. The "just tell us what you need" framing puts the design responsibility on us — a positive sign, and a real risk if we drift. Scope the presentation around Pay by Bank payout as the first concrete use case, with stablecoin off-ramp as the second.

BVNK

  • Counterpart: Ariane
  • Status: BVNK has offered to make their rails available to Aryze.
  • Agreed: formal email to Ariane requesting the next step. Owner: Jodi.

Strategic read: access to BVNK's rails would shorten time-to-market for stablecoin payment flows and reduce integration work. The relationship must respect Aryze's capability discipline: Aryze remains infrastructure to the customer, and BVNK rails sit as an underlying integration, not as a co-branded position. Worth clarifying the commercial model (revenue share, flat fee, volume tiers) before scoping the technical integration.

Mastercard

  • Counterpart: Christian Rau, VP
  • Status: Group meeting scheduled for Monday 25 May, with Utila.
  • Agreed: Jodi sets the meeting structure and circulates the agenda.

Strategic read: VP-level engagement at Mastercard is a strong opening. The Utila link points toward a wallet / issuance angle rather than a pure card-rails conversation. Position Aryze as infrastructure into the wallet-plus-card stack — not as a competing wallet. The capability boundaries in master CLAUDE.md apply here: Aryze does not issue, does not hold the licence, does not become the operator.

Ripple

  • Counterpart: Mindaugas Miskinis
  • Status: Product conversation requested.
  • Agreed: a follow-up product meeting. Owner: Jodi.

Strategic read: Ripple's product surface (RLUSD, On-Demand Liquidity, custody) overlaps with both Stablecoin Factory and Digital Cash. The first follow-up should clarify whether the angle is liquidity, settlement rails, or stablecoin issuance partnership — different routes lead to very different product conversations. A short internal pre-meeting between Rabii and Jodi to pick the lead angle will pay off.

2 · The pipeline — implications by product

Pay by Bank

Real and direct impact, on two fronts. First, Visa indicated they can help with the payout capability we need on the Pay by Bank side — the missing leg of the merchant proposition. Closing this gap would let us pitch a complete inbound-plus-payout flow to merchants in UK (live today via Volt.io) and EU/EEA (rollout late Q2 2026, pending Volt extension contract). Second, Paz van Gelder (Utila), met at the C-level dinner, engaged on an introducer arrangement built around his gaming-sector network — a direct iGaming pipeline lead into our primary ICP, with follow-up agreed in person in Stockholm. Beyond Visa and Paz, Stablecon is a stablecoin-led conference; Pay by Bank's natural demand-generation channels remain iGaming, betting and payment service provider (PSP) events (FIBE, Pay360, SBC).

Stablecoin Factory

Strongest pipeline contribution. BVNK rails, Visa push-function, and Mastercard / Utila could all become enabling partnerships. Quantoz and Dune sit in the builder / ecosystem layer and support go-to-market credibility more than near-term revenue.

Digital Cash

Rabobank, Ripple and the Utila-hosted institutional conversation are the relevant threads. Expect a long sales cycle: 6–18 months per ICP. Treat these as long-term relationship building rather than pipeline for the next quarter.

Discussion theme: where stablecoins and financial infrastructure are heading next.

Strategic read

The mix in the room — payments, fintech, banking and digital assets, at senior level — is exactly the audience Aryze needs for both Digital Cash institutional buyers and Stablecoin Factory builder customers. The Monday Mastercard / Utila meeting came directly out of this dinner relationship and shows the format works for Aryze.

4 · Assessment

An honest assessment of what the trip was worth:

  • Two real outcomes. BVNK offered their rails and Visa signalled it can help close the Pay by Bank payout gap — both concrete, and both with the follow-up sitting at Aryze at close.
  • The right level, early stage. All seven conversations ran at the right seniority — VP and C-level — but Mastercard, Ripple, Quantoz, Rabobank and Dune were openings, not agreements, each with a named owner at close.
  • Honest risks. Visa’s “tell us what you need” framing puts the design burden on Aryze; the BVNK offer has no commercial model behind it yet; and the customer threads run on long cycles — Digital Cash qualification is measured in months, not weeks.
  • Bottom line. The trip did the narrow job it was sent to do — identify the right partners and the right customers for all three product lines. The value now rests on how cleanly the two partner loops are closed.
