Executive summary

The SBC Summit produced a pipeline of 16 named contacts, organised in three tiers — and its value sits in distribution rather than in any single merchant. Several of the contacts are payment platforms, iGaming platforms, orchestration layers and affiliate networks, where one integration or one relationship can open several commercial opportunities at once. That makes the SBC outcome larger than a standard event lead list.

The floor also validated the positioning. Conversations confirmed demand for lower per-transaction cost, faster settlement and fewer chargebacks — the Pay by Bank pitch resonates with payment-driven iGaming operators.

The operational contact list — phone numbers, emails and what each contact agreed at the stand — lives in the pipeline appendix.

1 · On the ground — what the floor showed

Three observations, and the commercial leverage map behind them.

  1. The market validated the positioning. Demand for lower per-transaction cost, faster settlement and fewer chargebacks came up unprompted across conversations.
  2. The value is in distribution, not direct sales. Apcopay, GameBridge.io, Finnplay, BridgerPay and SoftConstruct are access points to multiple operators, not single-customer leads.
  3. Relationships need ownership and cadence. Line Ho Young Peteri, Jesper, Anne, Angela and Steve are strategic network relationships, not ad-hoc contacts.
  • Payment platforms — Apcopay, MaksuPay. Potential to add Pay by Bank as a payment method in existing setups: integrate once, reach their merchant book.
  • iGaming platforms — GameBridge.io, Finnplay. Platforms with operator-base access and clear payment pain: pilot through one operator, expand via the platform.
  • Orchestration and ecosystem — BridgerPay, SoftConstruct. Distribution layers where one integration can open several commercial paths at once.

3 · What became structure — distribution and the network

The durable outcome of the trip is a distribution thesis, not a contact count. Treated as a programme, the SBC pipeline gives Aryze access points where one integration creates several commercial opportunities: payment platforms that can carry Pay by Bank into their merchant books, iGaming platforms that can pilot through one operator and expand, and orchestration layers that open several paths at once.

Around the platforms sits a strategic network. Line Ho Young Peteri (ATFX) is a key relationship into the gaming, affiliate and partnership landscape — and the route into ATFX itself. Jesper, Anne, Angela and Steve form a partner network whose introductions carry real pipeline value, provided each relationship has a named owner and a simple cadence so the introductions never become person-dependent.

4 · Assessment

An honest assessment of what the trip was worth:

  • One real asset. A distribution-shaped pipeline — four platform routes at tier 1 and sixteen named contacts — worth more than its contact count if the integrations land.
  • Validated positioning. The Pay by Bank pitch held up on the floor: cost, settlement speed and chargebacks are the conversation iGaming payment teams want to have.
  • Honest risks. Decision mandate was unclear at several tier-2 contacts and needs confirming before any scoping; technical capacity should only follow commercial qualification; iGaming leads need MLRO and KYB review in parallel, not after; and coverage promises must stay inside the live footprint — UK live today, EU/EEA from late Q2 2026.
  • Bottom line. Event momentum decays fast — the value of this pipeline is decided by the speed and discipline of the first follow-up wave, while SBC is still a warm reference.

Where it stands

  • The 16 contacts are logged with the follow-up each agreed at the stand — full detail in the appendix.
  • Tier 1 — Apcopay, GameBridge.io, Finnplay and ATFX — is queued first for commercial discovery.
  • Tier 2 — MaksuPay, BridgerPay and SoftConstruct — is queued for qualification, with Emma Vassallo as the reference into BridgerPay.
  • The network relationships — Line, Jesper, Anne, Angela, Steve — are being placed under single named ownership so the introductions stay structural rather than personal.
