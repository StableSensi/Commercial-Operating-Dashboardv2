Executive summary

NEXT in Malta is one of Europe's largest iGaming and betting gatherings. A single day on the ground produced a concrete relationship at executive level, a broad operator pipeline — and something more durable: the start of an Aryze agent network in the Malta iGaming ecosystem.

The standout meeting was face to face with Nicolas Lund, COO of Nordplay — a direct, warm relationship at executive level with an active Nordic operator group. Through Louise Beyer Tingager, our introducer on site, we met a group of Malta-based operator and industry leaders, and we prioritised 20 operator connections at tier 2.

The trip also converted relationships into structure. Louise's introducer agreement was moved onto updated terms during the event itself (27 May). Victor Olinger, Co-CEO of DELULU Gaming, whom we met through Louise, took a follow-up call after the trip and accepted an introducer agreement on 5 June. Anthony and A B, both met on the ground in Malta, are working with us as agents — the first asks went out to them on 31 May.

1 · On the ground

Nicolas Lund — COO, Nordplay. The trip's strongest single meeting: face to face, opening a direct, warm relationship at executive level. Nordplay is a Nordic operator group (Buccone Trading Ltd / NordPlay Malta Ltd, HQ Birkirkara) running five brands on its Swedish licence — Frank & Fred, Klirr, Pop, Jubla and the Onerush sportsbook — with markets in Sweden, Finland and Estonia and roughly 40 employees. Their cashier already runs heavy on account-to-account rails, which makes the conversation concrete rather than educational. Follow-up was sent 31 May with a Money20/20 sit-down proposed.

Through Louise Beyer Tingager, our introducer on site, we also met a group of Malta-based operator and industry leaders — Victor Olinger, Co-CEO of DELULU Gaming (previously Head of Gaming at Vera&John), Christopher Dalli, CEO of L&L Europe, and others below. Emma Formosa gives us a second route into Nordplay.

Justinas Šliažas — 7bet. A planned in-person catch-up: 7bet runs its own group EMI for player processing and was clear that the group rails stay. The one open wedge, agreed before Malta, is Trustly's UK per-transaction cost — if 7bet pays more than GBP 0.50 per transaction in the UK, there is a conversation.

4 · Assessment

An honest assessment of what the trip was worth:

  • Two real assets. The Nordplay relationship at executive level — and an agent track that turned event relationships into signed and offered introducer agreements within a week.
  • A credible funnel, not yet value. The 20 are connections, not conversations; their worth depends on follow-up over the coming two weeks, while NEXT is still a warm reference.
  • Full UK/EU reach. All 20 are in UK and EU operator markets, so we can start outreach right away.
  • Honest risks. The list is drawn from the event app and not yet verified company-by-company; most relationships are single-threaded; and the Money20/20 sit-down is proposed, not booked.
  • Bottom line. A strong networking trip with one genuine relationship and a list worth following up — roughly 7–8/10 on outcome, with the score decided by follow-up discipline over the next two weeks.

Where it stands

  • Nordplay: follow-up sent 31 May; a Money20/20 sit-down proposed and awaiting Nicolas's confirmation.
  • The agent track: Louise on updated terms; Victor's agreement accepted and in paperwork; Anthony and A B active with first asks out.
  • The 20: handed into the junior-seller motion — attendee list reviewed, outreach running across UK/EU operators.
