Booth activities

The activity strategy is built on one idea: create visible energy that pulls in passersby, generates a natural queue, and gives the team time to qualify leads while visitors wait.

Mini golf — "The Payment Race"

Two-lane mini golf. One lane is blocked with obstacles representing old payment friction (delays, fees, failed transactions). The other is clear — straight to the hole — representing Pay by Bank. Both run simultaneously.

  • Obstacle lane: barriers labelled with bank delays, FX friction, chargebacks
  • Clear lane: fast, unobstructed — "The Aryze Way"
  • Leaderboard on tablet — fastest time wins an Aryze Viking
  • New round starts every hour — creates urgency and a queue
  • One team member runs the activity; the other works the queue to qualify leads

Live demo — Pay by Bank & FX swap

Live product demo running at the booth at all times. Visitors can input a real scenario and see settlement speed versus traditional rails side by side. The demo is the conversation starter — not the close.

Lead capture

Before playing the mini golf challenge, attendees sign up via a dedicated Aryze-branded webpage on a tablet at the booth. The form collects the fields below and submits directly to the NBC Stockholm contact list in Brevo. On successful submission, a confirmation screen is shown, and the attendee is directed to the golf station. The webpage is built ahead of the conference.

  • Hot lead (decision-maker, clear problem to solve): a call is booked before they leave the booth
  • Warm lead (right company, wrong role): flagged for follow-up
  • Cold lead: added to the Brevo newsletter list

Post-event follow-up

  • All leads uploaded to Brevo CRM by evening of 27 May
  • Email 1 (within 48 hours): personal note + relevant one-pager attached
  • Email 2 (day 3): product deep-dive link
  • Email 3 (day 7): soft call to action — demo call or webinar invite
