Commercial process · Lead pipeline

The lead pipeline

The pipeline shows where each lead sits, who owns it, which product path it belongs to and what happens next. The board carries status and movement, not prose.

Owner: Commercial · 2026 · Confidential

Pipeline rule

Status, owner and next step must be visible. If those three things are unclear, the lead card is not ready for review.

The board reads from left to right

Each column is a stage. A lead card moves right as the lead becomes more concrete. Side stages keep long-term, parked or closed leads out of the active sales flow.

The stage signals what kind of action a lead needs

The lead card is the quick status view

A lead card is readable without opening it. It shows who the lead is, who owns it internally, which company it belongs to, the product path, any agent or referral, and the next task.

Lead card fields

  • Contact is the person or lead name at the top of the card.
  • Owner is the internal Aryze person responsible for follow-up.
  • Company shows the organisation behind the lead.
  • Product shows the likely path: Pay by Bank, Digital Cash, Stablecoin Factory or another relevant route.
  • Agent captures the referral or introduction source when relevant.
  • Task is the next concrete action, such as follow-up, meeting booking or waiting for response.

Notes, Tasks and Files hold the detail behind the card

Opening a lead card reveals the tabs that carry the working context. The board stays clean; the detail lives inside the lead.

  • Notes — status and history. What happened, what was sent, what the lead answered and what comes next.
  • Tasks — the next action. Follow-up, booking, sending material, checking with an agent or waiting for a response.
  • Files — material shared or received. One-pagers, decks, agreements, screenshots or any document needed to understand the lead.
