Where the cost actually sits

Settlement still runs on rails built for end-of-day reconciliation. The visible cost is correspondent banking fees, still 5–8% in many corridors on T+2 windows. The structural cost is harder to see on the P&L: intraday liquidity tied up in nostro and suspense accounts, settlement risk across counterparties, and operational headcount sitting on reconciliation queues that exist only because the rails cannot net in real time.

For groups operating across multiple jurisdictions, the drag is permanent. The same flow moves through the same friction every business day.

A settlement layer that nets in real time

Tokenised settlement removes the reconciliation cycle from the value movement. Transactions settle on-ledger between participants under your governance, on your rulebook, with finality you define.

Banks that built this internally took 12 to 24 months and a fragmented vendor stack across custody, ledger, on-ramps, and compliance tooling. Consortium routes solve the problem for member banks on the consortium's timeline. Aryze delivers the operating layer for institutions that want to settle independently, on their own.

What you get

Real-time settlement and clearing between participants under your governance and your settlement-finality framework.

Issuance of the settlement asset under your own licence. Your rules, your counterparty rulebook, your audit trail. Aryze provides the infrastructure behind it.

Multi-currency on one operating layer. A single rail for cross-jurisdiction groups, with on-ledger movement between currencies.

Interoperability across networks and counterparties. Private and public DLT, with integration into the wider ecosystem when you choose to participate.

On-ramps to and from the regulated banking system. Delivered through our Mastercard Open Banking partnership, with reach across 2,900+ banks in 19 European markets.

Integration with your existing stack. Core banking, treasury management, ledger, and wallet systems. ISO 20022, FATF, and MiCA aligned. AML and sanctions screening built in.
