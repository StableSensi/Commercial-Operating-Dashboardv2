Brutal navigation test

"If a new Aryze employee opened the workspace today, could they navigate it independently within 20 minutes?"

Answer: Yes, with two caveats.

Why yes:

  1. Master CLAUDE.md opens with an "If you are Claude and just walked in" section that gives a 4-step reading order plus 3 override rules. A new human follows the same path.
  2. Every top-level folder has its own CLAUDE.md explaining contents, structure, and naming conventions.
  3. Personas, frameworks, and templates are clearly separated in Brain/. Each sub-folder has an index.
  4. Products are consistently mapped across the workspace — Pay by Bank / Stablecoin Factory / Digital Cash mentioned by canonical name in master CLAUDE, persona files, foundation docs, and project CLAUDE files. The audit-flagged wrong product names ("Aryze Factory", "Digital Currency") no longer appear in active material.
  5. Frameworks and templates cross-reference correctly — Discovery-Call.md points to specialist personas; Qualification-Framework.md points to per-product analyses; VPC-Method.md points to per-product VPC files.
  6. Open questions are visible — each persona's §9 lists what's not yet decided. A new employee can see at a glance which boundaries are firm and which are still ambiguous.

Caveat 1: The Analyser/ files are still in Danish.

Documents/Analyser/Pay-by-Bank/ICP.md, VPC.md, Product-Description.md (and the equivalents for Stablecoin Factory and Digital Cash) remain in Danish. A new English-only employee can still navigate the workspace because the master CLAUDE.md, all personas, all frameworks, all templates, all folder CLAUDE.md files, and the per-product CLAUDE.md indexes are in English — and these point to the Danish source documents. But the source documents themselves haven't been translated.

This is a deliberate scope choice — translating the Analyser/ files was outside this restructure's scope. Recommend translating in a separate sprint when capacity allows.

Caveat 2: Open questions need Stablesensei input.

Each persona has a §9 with precise open questions. Without Stablesensei's input on those, the personas operate at 85–90% — not 100%. The remaining 10–15% is real ambiguity (newsletter status, X presence, approved testimonials, exec voice models, pricing tier table, supported networks list, compliance pack location, etc.).

These don't block navigation, but they will surface during actual sales / marketing work. Recommend Stablesensei does a single 90-minute review covering all six persona §9 sections to close them.
