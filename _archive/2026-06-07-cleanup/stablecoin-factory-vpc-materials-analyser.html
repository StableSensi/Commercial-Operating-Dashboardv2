Stablecoin Factory · Value Proposition Canvas

Why builders choose Stablecoin Factory

The customer's jobs, pains and gains on one side; what we deliver and how it relieves them on the other. Factory is the deployment layer — the customer remains issuer, and full institutional operation is Digital Cash.

Owner: Commercial · Confidential · Structure follows the Strategyzer canvas

The customer's jobs

Functional: deploy a stablecoin or tokenised asset from concept to live; test the use case in practice before deeper commitment; validate product-market fit; iterate on design and parameters (token model, supply mechanism, custody, networks); integrate the deployment with their own product or partner platform; keep governance and operational control.

Professional: show investors, board and partners that the use case runs live; demonstrate technical maturity; differentiate from competitors still in concept mode.

Emotional: the feeling of moving from "we are exploring" to "we are live"; trust that the infrastructure layer is not a black box; pride in a deployment that did not take six months of custom build; the sense that it is still the customer's product, not Aryze's.

The pains

TypePain
Time to liveThe path from idea to live is too heavy — too many decisions, vendors and internal alignment. A custom build costs months before there is anything to show. Enterprise sales cycles are too long to validate a use case in time.
CostCustom infrastructure is expensive — engineers, a vendor stack and integration time. Enterprise vendors price for institutional use, not exploratory deployment.
ComplexityA fragmented stack: wallet, ledger, custody, ramps, compliance — all separate. Technical decisions are hard without domain experience. Compliance, legal and tech each take their own time, with no clear order.
RiskTime and capital invested without knowing whether the use case works. Technical choices can prove wrong and force redeployment. The regulatory landscape shifts (MiCA, US legislation).
OutcomeWithout a clear starting point the project sits in concept mode, and the team loses investor and internal momentum. Without a path to bigger infrastructure they cannot answer "what happens if it grows".

The gains

LevelGain
RequiredA fast, clear route from concept to live. Enough customisation to match the use case. A self-serve experience without an enterprise sales cycle. A clear issuer/operator model — the customer owns it.
ExpectedSandbox and documentation the technical team can evaluate alone. Low-friction issuance. Branding control. A modern, assisted deployment flow.
DesiredA path up to Digital Cash if the use case grows institutional. Real-world learning. Partner and white-label options.
UnexpectedAssisted setup that cuts manual work. A migration path that is real, not a marketing claim. Credibility from being on Aryze infrastructure — a Copenhagen fintech with Mastercard rails, not a token experiment.

What we deliver

  • The Stablecoin Factory deployment layer — self-serve infrastructure for simple stablecoin and tokenised-asset deployment
  • A self-serve deployment flow — concept to live without enterprise complexity on day one
  • Sandbox and documentation — the technical team evaluates before any commercial commitment
  • Three commercial motions — own programme, partner-led distribution, white-label enablement
  • A path up to Digital Cash when the use case outgrows the deployment layer
  • Issuer preservation — the customer remains issuer under their own licence

How that relieves the pains

PainRelief
Time to liveSelf-serve flow: weeks to a quarter instead of 6–12 months. Pre-built infrastructure — no custom build from scratch.
CostOne coherent infrastructure layer replaces the fragmented vendor stack. Self-serve pricing instead of an enterprise contract. Less internal engineering burden.
ComplexityOne deployment flow replaces many vendors. A pre-validated technical model means fewer custom decisions. Documentation and sandbox let the team learn on their own.
RiskTest and learn before deeper commitment. The migration path to Digital Cash means no rebuild when the use case grows. The customer stays issuer — a clean legal position.
OutcomeA visible deployment path moves the project out of concept mode, and gives a clear line to investors and the board: "we are live on Aryze."

What we do not deliver — say it early

  • Aryze does not become the issuer — the customer remains issuer under their own licence (or a partner / parent licensed entity).
  • Aryze does not become the regulated operator — the customer operates the programme.
  • Factory is not Digital Cash — full institutional operation (settlement, ramps, treasury, multi-currency, governance) routes to Digital Cash.
  • No custom enterprise programmes from day one — if the customer wants a custom build, re-route or disqualify.
  • RWA capabilities beyond the one-pagers, custody guarantees, and Digital Cash features on Factory pricing — verify with product, per deal.
  • Licence delivery — Aryze does not deliver the licence the customer operates under.
