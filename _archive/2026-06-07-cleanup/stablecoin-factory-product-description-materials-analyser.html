Stablecoin Factory · Product description

What Stablecoin Factory is — and is not

A self-serve infrastructure product for launching simple stablecoins and tokenised assets through a low-friction deployment flow. The simple deployment layer in the Aryze suite — not institutional operation, and never the issuer.

Owner: Commercial · Confidential

Overview

Stablecoin Factory is built for teams that want to move from concept to live deployment without a long enterprise process, a large custom build, or the full complexity of institutional digital-cash infrastructure. It helps teams launch, test and learn faster — a practical way to start before they know whether the use case needs a deeper operating model.

Two lines that must never blur: Factory is simple deployment; Digital Cash is institutional operation. And Factory is infrastructure — Aryze does not become issuer or regulated operator; that role stays with the customer or a partner / parent licensed entity.

In one sentence

A self-serve infrastructure layer that lets teams launch a simple stablecoin or tokenised asset without starting with enterprise complexity on day one.

Who it is for

Builders, technical teams, innovation teams, product teams, digital-asset teams and exploratory operators — typically with a stablecoin or tokenisation idea or early use case, who want speed, usability and a clear deployment path. Full profile: the ICP.

The problem it solves

Many teams can see a stablecoin or RWA use case, but getting from idea to live deployment is still too hard: too many technical decisions, infrastructure components, vendors, legal questions and internal discussions. Teams can spend months scoping before testing anything real.

The issue is not only that deployment is complex — it is that the complexity arrives too early. Some teams just need a simple way to launch, test and learn. Without a clear starting point, stablecoin and RWA projects sit stuck in concept mode.

The value proposition

"A faster way to launch a simple stablecoin."

Factory helps teams move from concept to deployment faster, cut early custom-build work, avoid a premature enterprise process, deploy through a usable self-serve flow, test the use case in practice, learn from real use, and start simple before moving into deeper infrastructure needs.

What we deliver — confirmed

  • Self-serve deployment flow — low friction, assisted setup
  • An issuance path for a simple stablecoin or tokenised asset
  • Documentation and sandbox for technical evaluation without a sales process
  • Three go-to-market motions (below)
  • A path up to Digital Cash if the use case scales
  • The issuer model stays with the customer

What we do not deliver — be explicit

  • Aryze does not become the issuer — the customer remains issuer under their own licence (or a partner / parent licensed entity).
  • Aryze does not become the regulated operator — the customer operates.
  • Factory is not Digital Cash — full institutional operation routes to Digital Cash.
  • No custom enterprise programmes from day one.
  • RWA capabilities beyond the one-pagers — verify with product; the product anchors in stablecoins.
  • Specific networks, jurisdictions and custody partners — confirm per deal with product.
  • The Factory → Digital Cash migration — a path, not a productised step; the technical migration depends on the use case.
  • Licence delivery — Aryze does not deliver the licence.

How it is sold

A product-led and ecosystem-led motion — different from both Pay by Bank (commercial merchant motion) and Digital Cash (enterprise institutional motion). Channels: the self-serve product funnel, developer communities, digital-asset events (Token2049, Permissionless, EthCC, RWA events), technical walkthroughs and demos, founder-led conversations, stablecoin/RWA content, and partner channels for the partner-led and white-label motions.

The typical path: inbound or a warm introduction → a qualifying call (use case, motion fit, technical maturity) → technical walkthrough or sandbox access → solution scoping → commercial framing per motion → deployment → and, when relevant, the path to scale into Digital Cash.

Pricing

Pricing varies per motion — own programme, partner-led and white-label have different economics, commitments and success criteria. Seller rule: do not quote on a first call. "Once we know which motion fits and what scope you are after, I will come back with framing."
