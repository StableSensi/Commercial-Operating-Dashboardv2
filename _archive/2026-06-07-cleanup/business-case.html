1. Basis of the model

This case takes the market inputs from the assessment (venue counts, average transaction values, the competitive picture) and builds a view of revenue potential, cost and payback. Figures are calculated from published sector data and the planning assumptions set out below, and labelled as fact, assumption or estimate. The pilot replaces the estimates with measured numbers.

Against card acquiring the fixed fee wins at realistic ticket sizes. Against the cheapest account-to-account (A2A) specialists it wins as the ticket rises (above roughly EUR 70 against a 0.6% rate). 3. Revenue model

Method

Per-venue revenue equals estimated annual transactions multiplied by the fixed fee, multiplied by the share of payment flow Aryze captures. Annual transactions are estimated from published sector revenue divided by average transaction value, with an assumption on transactions per visit. How much of a guest's spend lands in one transaction (a drink, a round, a tab, or a top-up) depends on how the venue integrates the rail, which is the venue's decision. The market inputs are taken from the market assessment.

Platform opportunity (the dominant variable)

Festival cashless platforms aggregate volume across many events and currently run their top-up flow on card rails. A single platform relationship is larger than any national venue-level rollout.

ScenarioEstimated annual revenue to Aryze at 30% capture
One platform across 20 mid-sized festivalsEUR 2 to 8 million
One platform across 50 plus festivals at scaleEUR 5 to 20 million

All figures are estimates, and timing matters: the 2026 festival season is already contracted, so any platform revenue is a 2027 outcome. The work now is to open the relationships that land in 2027.

6. Sensitivity: what moves the outcome

  • Platform deal, yes or no. This is the single biggest swing. A venue-level business is steady and profitable on its own. With a platform relationship at scale it is an order of magnitude larger.
  • Capture rate. Moving from 10 to 30% triples per-venue revenue. It is an assumption until a pilot measures it.
  • Ticket size and flow. The model is strongest on tabs and top-ups, where the effective rate is lowest. The realised average ticket sets the effective rate, ranging from around 2% on a round to under 1% on a tab.
  • Pricing tier. The DKK 2 to 2.5 mainstream tier changes competitiveness against other A2A providers but lowers per-transaction revenue.

7. Cost and resourcing (commercial view)

This is the commercial read, not a full cost model. Summer execution is a small commercial team running direct venue pilots in Denmark and the UK. The two costs the pilot confirms are the cost to serve a venue (onboarding, compliance, integration support) and the real CAC.

8. Risks to the numbers

  • The volume and revenue figures are estimates from published data, not primary venue data.
  • The platform revenue, which carries the upside, is unproven for Aryze and is a 2027 timing question.
  • The CAC and cost to serve are benchmarks, not measured.
  • Pricing is an assumption pending internal sign-off.
  • The case scales through breadth across many venues and a platform relationship, rather than resting on any single venue.

10. The economic verdict

The economics work, with a clear shape. Per-venue payback is fast and well inside industry norms even on conservative assumptions, so venue-level activity is profitable and scales across the estate. The case becomes materially larger with a platform relationship, which is the largest opportunity and a 2027 outcome. The pilot confirms the model with live numbers.
