What a VPC tells you

The canvas splits in half:

Customer Profile (what the buyer experiences):

  • Jobs — what they're trying to get done (functional, social, emotional)
  • Pains — what goes wrong today: cost, operational burden, risk, missed conversion, internal friction
  • Gains — what they want: required (must-have), expected (table-stakes), desired (would be nice), unexpected (surprise)

Value Map (what Aryze offers):

  • Products & Services — what we actually deliver
  • Pain Relievers — how each capability addresses each pain
  • Gain Creators — how each capability delivers each gain category

A good pitch is the value map mapped to the customer profile — not the other way round. We don't start with our features and look for matching pains. We start with their pains and ask which of our capabilities relieves them.

How to use VPC in a discovery call

  1. Open with the customer's job: "What are you trying to get done today? What's the outcome that matters?"
  2. Probe for pains using the customer's language, not yours. If they say "scheme fees are killing us", use "scheme fees" — not "card costs".
  3. Quantify when you can: "How much has that gone up? How many transactions a month? What does that cost you per quarter?"
  4. Map their pains to your relievers in real time. Don't list features. Pick the one capability that lands on the specific pain they just described.
  5. Ask which gain category matters most: required (must-haves), expected (table-stakes), desired (would-love), unexpected (didn't-know-was-possible). Required and desired drive deals.

Anchor every commercial line to a pain or gain

The rule

If a sentence in your outreach, deck, or proposal doesn't trace back to either a Pain Reliever or a Gain Creator from the VPC — cut it.

This rule is the single most effective filter for keeping Aryze writing on-voice. It is documented as voice discipline in the Writer persona (Brain/Personas/Writer/CLAUDE.md, §4.2 Concrete over abstract) and applied in the audit report (Documents/Reports/Audit-Modsigelser-2026-05-13.md).

Don't blur

Each VPC maps a distinct customer profile to a distinct value map. Pay by Bank's VPC is not Stablecoin Factory's VPC. Mixing them loses the deal: pitching account-to-account (A2A) merchant rails to a builder who wants to deploy a stablecoin, or pitching self-serve deployment to a bank that needs institutional settlement.

The discipline is documented in Documents/Analyser/README.md:

"Anchor every commercial line to a pain or gain. If you can't trace a line back to the VPC — cut it. Match the VPC to the buyer role. Don't blur products."

Match the VPC to the buyer role

The same product is sold differently to different buyers. The VPC stays the same, but the emphasis changes:

  • Payments Operations Lead (Pay by Bank): operational fit, integration path, exception handling, control
  • CFO / Finance Director (Pay by Bank): predictable cost, EBITDA impact, cash flow
  • Founder / CTO (Stablecoin Factory): time-to-live, sandbox quality, technical evaluation
  • Executive Sponsor (Digital Cash): strategic narrative, board defensibility, peer positioning
  • MLRO / General Counsel (Digital Cash): regulatory framing, compliance hooks, audit trail

Buyer-specific pitch angles live in each specialist persona under Brain/Personas/Sales-[Product]/.
