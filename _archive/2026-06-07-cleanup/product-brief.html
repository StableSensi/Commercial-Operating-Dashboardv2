Product Brief · Rail & Integration

Pay by Bank for nightlife: rail and integration

What Aryze sells in this vertical — the chargeback-free account-to-account rail, the venue's integration choices, the flows it supports, the electronic-money boundary and what a pilot needs from it.

Owner: Product · 30 May 2026 · Confidential

↩ Package overview Source .md

Date: 30 May 2026

1. What Aryze sells

Aryze sells one thing in this vertical: the Pay by Bank rail. It is a chargeback-free, account-to-account (A2A) payment that pushes funds from the guest's own bank to the venue's bank under strong customer authentication, running on Mastercard Open Banking. There is no card scheme in the flow, so there is no card chargeback and no rolling reserve. Funds settle quickly to the venue's own account, and venues are onboarded with know-your-business (KYB) checks on the same basis as the casinos Aryze already serves.

That is the product. It is a rail, not an app, a till or a wallet.

2. Integration is the venue's decision

The venue decides how to put the rail in front of its guests, whether through a QR code at the point of sale, a tab or top-up flow, or a loyalty wallet. Aryze provides the same rail behind all of these, and does not build the venue's app, choose its point-of-sale hardware, or run its loyalty programme.

3. The flows the rail supports

The rail moves money one way: from the guest to the venue. That covers a bar or checkout payment, settling a tab, and topping up a venue's own wallet or account. Payouts are not a standalone Aryze flow.

Because the fee is fixed per transaction, the economics are strongest on tabs and top-ups, where the effective rate is lowest. The market analysis and the business case set out what each flow means for the numbers.

4. The wallet and electronic-money boundary

If a venue or a platform offers a stored-balance wallet (a pre-loaded balance the guest spends down), that balance is electronic money, and a Danish provider was challenged by the consumer ombudsman on exactly this point. In that case the wallet and the electronic-money role sit with the venue or its electronic-money partner. Aryze stays the rail beneath it, pushing funds from the guest's bank into the wallet, and never holds the balance itself.

5. What a pilot needs from the rail

To produce the proof the business case needs, a pilot has to show the rail working at a real venue: a live A2A payment, fast settlement to the venue's account, confirmation that no card-scheme chargeback applies, and simple reporting on transactions, value and timing. The venue chooses the front end it puts the rail behind. Aryze supplies the rail and the data.

6. Aryze's commitment

Aryze's commitment is the rail: the A2A payment, the settlement, the compliance onboarding, and the reporting behind it.
