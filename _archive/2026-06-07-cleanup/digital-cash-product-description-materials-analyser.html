Digital Cash · Product description

What Digital Cash is — and is not

Enterprise infrastructure for already-licensed institutions that want to issue and operate digital cash under their own licence, governance and control. The most enterprise-grade product in the suite — not a self-serve entry product.

Owner: Commercial · Confidential

Overview

Digital Cash is built for institutions that need more than simple issuance. These customers are not looking for a token demo — they need the operating infrastructure required to make digital cash work in real financial environments: settlement, on/off-ramps, treasury functionality, multi-currency issuance, interoperability, and asset movement across environments.

The positioning must be precise: Aryze delivers infrastructure. The customer remains issuer and operator under their own licence. That distinction is central. Digital Cash should never be sold to customers who only need a lightweight stablecoin test — that is Stablecoin Factory.

In one sentence

Enterprise operating infrastructure for already-licensed institutions to issue and operate digital cash under their own licence, governance and control.

Who it is for

Already-licensed institutions with a concrete digital cash, settlement, treasury, liquidity or value-movement use case: banks exploring or running tokenised deposits, banks handling intra-bank settlement, licensed financial institutions, fintech clusters needing clearing between participants, licensed conglomerates, institutions with treasury or liquidity needs, and — rarely but strategically — central banks with wholesale CBDC experiments. Full profile: the ICP.

The problem it solves

For institutions, issuing a digital asset is only part of the problem — the harder part is operating it properly. Simple issuance does not solve settlement, liquidity, treasury, ramps, compliance, auditability, interoperability, reporting or integration with existing systems. A digital cash programme can also require approval from treasury, legal, compliance, risk, operations, technology, senior management and sometimes the board. The core problem is the gap between digital-cash ambition and operational readiness.

The value proposition

"The infrastructure layer for institutions that want to operate digital cash properly."

Digital Cash helps institutions issue under their own licence, keep governance and operational control, support settlement use cases and on/off-ramps, handle treasury functionality, issue in multiple currencies, move assets across environments, improve interoperability, reduce the burden of building the full stack internally, and move from concept or pilot toward live operation.

What we do not deliver — be explicit

  • Aryze does not hold the customer's licence and takes on no regulated activity
  • Aryze does not become the issuer — the institution issues
  • Aryze does not operate the programme — the institution operates under its own licence
  • No "live in six weeks" — the cycle is 6–18 months; do not sell speed
  • No "plug-and-play" — saying it destroys credibility with the buying committee
  • No consortium building — that is Fnality/Partior territory; we serve single institutions
  • No central-bank functions and no licence delivery
  • Specific networks, jurisdictions and custody partners — confirm per deal with product

How it is sold

An enterprise sale: long cycle, wide committee, high consideration. Channels: direct institutional outreach, warm introductions via advisors, lawyers, regulators and industry bodies, institutional events (Sibos, Money20/20, Point Zero, Digital Assets Week), strategic partnerships, inbound RFPs and regulator-driven introductions.

The typical path: multi-touch discovery (surface the use case, confirm the licence, identify the executive sponsor) → a workshop with treasury, compliance and technology in the room → solution architecture against the existing stack → commercial framing with phased deployment → legal, risk and procurement (expect months) → pilot or phase-1 deployment → live operation and expansion. 6–18 months is normal. Do not sell speed.

Language rules for Digital Cash

  • Always: frame Aryze as the infrastructure provider; acknowledge regulatory complexity instead of waving it away; use operational, institutional language — settlement, liquidity, governance, auditability — not Web3 jargon.
  • Never: "we'll issue the digital cash for you" · "Aryze becomes your operator" · "live in weeks" · "plug-and-play" · "stablecoin" used loosely — institutions can dislike the term; follow the customer's language: digital cash, tokenised deposits, settlement asset.

Pricing

Priced as an enterprise infrastructure engagement, depending on scope (settlement only / treasury / multi-currency / full), volume framing, integration depth and the multi-year commitment. Seller rule: never quote a number on the first call — "let me come back with framing once we have scoped the use case and integration."
