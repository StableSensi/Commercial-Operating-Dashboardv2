Pay by Bank · Value Proposition Canvas

Why merchants buy Pay by Bank

The customer's jobs, pains and gains on one side; what we deliver and how it relieves them on the other. Covers incoming payments only (customer → merchant) — the direction Pay by Bank serves.

Owner: Commercial · Confidential · Structure follows the Strategyzer canvas

The customer's jobs

Functional: receive incoming payments reliably and fast; keep payment cost predictable and under control; reduce dispute and chargeback burden; reconcile incoming funds cleanly; stay compliant with scheme and regulatory demands (PSD2, VAMP, KYC/AML); run working deposit, top-up and customer-funding flows.

Professional: report payment performance to leadership and the board; stand behind the payment stack in front of risk, finance and engineering; negotiate better terms with acquirers and schemes.

Emotional: feel in control of where the money sits and when; trust that the rails will not suddenly fail (acquirer pull, scheme pressure); run a payment setup that costs no more than it should.

The pains

TypePain
CostCard fees scale with the amount — margin squeezed at high average tickets. Scheme fees up 75%+ since 2021. Loaded cost per dispute around 2.5× the transaction value in high-risk sectors.
OperationalChargebacks and disputes eat finance and risk time (iGaming: 2–4% chargeback rates against 0.5–1% in normal e-commerce). Refund handling is manual and fragmented. Reconciliation creates monthly work. Payment status is hard to see end to end.
RiskThe Visa VAMP threshold drops to 1.5% in April 2026 — existing chargeback rates threaten scheme relationships. Acquirers can pull card service in regulated categories. Fraud and dispute exposure.
ConversionCard details at checkout create friction. 3DS pop-ups drop conversion. Card declines hit legitimate customers.

The gains

LevelGain
RequiredPredictable, controlled payment cost. Settlement fast enough for cash flow. Falling chargeback exposure. Visibility on every payment from initiation to settlement.
ExpectedIntegration without a checkout rebuild. Theming and branding control on the payment screen. Stable coverage in their operating markets.
DesiredRecurring and refund flows on the same rail. Account-level data for verification and insights. 24/7 support and SLAs that match operations.
UnexpectedHigher conversion where A2A beats cards on speed. Lower fraud-team burden. Negotiating power with the acquirer once A2A is a real option.

What we deliver

  • The Pay by Bank A2A rail — on Mastercard Open Banking infrastructure: 19 European markets, 2,900+ banks. Customer → merchant only (checkout, deposits, top-ups, customer funding).
  • Fixed fee per transaction — not a percentage of the amount.
  • RememberMe — a token valid for 15 months. Repeat depositors approve in roughly half the clicks of a first deposit (about 50% faster repeat flow). A direct conversion gain on repeat deposits.
  • Theming and custom branding at checkout — logo, colours, font — so the merchant's brand carries through.
  • KYB + integration + go-live package — we map the customer's flow, run the integration and support go-live.
  • The PISP frame via Mastercard's licence — we operate on the partner's licence in the UK and EU under Open Banking / PSD2.
  • Sandbox and savings calculator for evaluation and a first estimate on the customer's own numbers.
  • 99.99%+ uptime on the Mastercard Open Banking platform — geo-redundancy, automatic failover, 24/7 support.

How that relieves the pains

PainRelief
CostFixed fee per transaction — cost no longer scales with the amount. A2A bypasses scheme-fee increases. The payment line becomes predictable to budget.
OperationalA2A is irrevocable and SCA-approved → near-zero chargebacks → less dispute burden. Direct bank-to-bank moves visibility close to real time. Cleaner reconciliation through direct payment data.
RiskA lower chargeback rate creates room under the Visa VAMP threshold. Less dependence on a single acquirer.
ConversionNo card details at checkout. No 3DS pop-up — strong customer authentication happens directly in the customer's bank. RememberMe makes repeat payments faster.

How that creates the gains

  • Predictable cost structure — finance gets budget control.
  • Better payment economics for high-volume merchants.
  • Mastercard-rail credibility — not a startup experiment.
  • Theming depth — the merchant keeps brand consistency.
  • Sandbox + savings calculator — we show numbers on the customer's own data before they commit.
  • The PISP frame and UK partner licence — the compliance story is ready.

What we do not deliver — say it early

We are PIS only and cover the customer → merchant direction. These jobs we do not solve:

  • AIS features: account owner verification, balance checks, account aggregation, spending insights, KYC via open banking, combined AIS+PIS flows. If the customer needs these, they must come from elsewhere.
  • Payouts (merchant → customer): withdrawals and standalone refunds are not productised at Aryze. If the customer wants the full cycle, we take the deposit side now and flag payouts as open.
  • Standalone recurring: not possible under PSD2 — only via Mastercard Payment Services DK/NO. Standalone expected with PSD3 (estimated 2027–28).
  • Refunds in Aryze's setup: always check with product before promising refund flows.
