Digital Cash · Value Proposition Canvas

Why institutions choose Digital Cash

The institution's jobs, pains and gains on one side; what we deliver and how it relieves them on the other. Digital Cash is the operating layer — the institution remains issuer and operator under its own licence.

Owner: Commercial · Confidential · Structure follows the Strategyzer canvas

The institution's jobs

Functional: issue digital cash under their own licence and governance; run programmes that meet treasury, settlement, liquidity or value-movement needs; settle transactions on-ledger with credible operational backing; manage liquidity and reserves on a token basis; operate on/off-ramps to traditional payments; integrate with existing core banking, treasury management, ledger and wallet systems; move assets across networks and counterparties; issue in multiple currencies.

Professional: satisfy internal stakeholders — legal, compliance, risk, treasury, technology — with credibility and operational depth; stand behind the programme in front of regulators, the board and the risk committee; demonstrate institutional capability to peer banks and counterparties.

Emotional: confidence that the programme stands up to regulator scrutiny; trust that the infrastructure provider is not a single point of failure; pride in a credible operating model rather than a proof of concept; control — it is the institution's programme, not Aryze's.

The pains

TypePain
Operating modelIssuance is not operation. Without an operating story, pilots strand and never reach live; the risk committee does not approve.
BuildAn internal build is slow (12–24+ months), expensive (5+ engineers) and complex. The vendor stack is fragmented, integration risk is high, and few people have built an institutional digital-cash stack before.
Governance & complianceStakeholders demand a credible operating model, not a marketing deck. AML/KYC/sanctions screening must be built in. Auditability and reporting requirements are strict. The legal structure is complex.
IntegrationCore banking (Temenos, Finastra, Avaloq, in-house), treasury systems (Kyriba, Bloomberg), ledger engines and wallet providers must all connect — legacy infrastructure has to be respected.
ControlThey want control without building everything internally; they fear vendor lock-in and resist outsourcing regulated activity.
StrategicPeer pressure from banks announcing tokenised deposits; the risk of being left behind; no clear story for the board; programmes drown without an executive sponsor.

The gains

LevelGain
RequiredControlled issuance under their own licence. Full operational infrastructure behind the token. A credible operating model they can explain to regulators and the board. Integration with existing systems. Settlement, ramps, treasury and multi-currency capability.
ExpectedA multi-year infrastructure partnership, not a transactional vendor. Governance hooks — controls, approvals, audit trails. Reporting integrations. Standards conformance (ISO 20022, FATF, MiCA where relevant). Mastercard-related on-ramp services. 24/7 institutional support.
DesiredInteroperability across networks and counterparties. Asset movement across private and public DLT. A clear migration story from Stablecoin Factory. Configurable token design per use case.

What we deliver

  • The Digital Cash operating infrastructure — settlement, on/off-ramps, treasury functionality, multi-currency issuance, interoperability, asset movement and governance hooks in one stack
  • On/off-ramps between digital cash and traditional payments, via the Mastercard Open Banking partnership
  • Governance hooks — controls, approvals and audit trails that match the institution's governance model
  • Reporting and integration with existing treasury, banking, wallet and ledger systems
  • Enterprise SLA and 24/7 institutional-grade support
  • A multi-year infrastructure partnership — partner, not transactional vendor

How that relieves the pains

PainRelief
Operating modelThe operating story is built into the infrastructure — settlement, ramps, treasury and multi-currency form one coherent stack. Pilots can move to live operation without re-platforming. The risk committee sees a credible operating model.
BuildOne coherent operating layer replaces the multi-vendor stack. Pre-built infrastructure cuts the custom-build burden. The timeline matches institutional reality — months, not weeks, but not two-plus years.
GovernanceAML/KYC/sanctions hooks, auditability and reporting are native, not bolted on. The customer remains issuer and operator — a clean legal position toward regulators.
IntegrationIntegrations with core banking, treasury, ledger and wallet providers are part of the infrastructure, not a post-deployment task. Standards conformance cuts regulatory friction. Mastercard on-ramp integration avoids separate vendor onboarding.
ControlAryze is infrastructure provider, not programme operator — the institution keeps control, with a preserved migration path instead of lock-in.
StrategicA story the institution can stand behind in front of the board, a credible partner for the executive sponsor to present, and peer positioning against banks that build alone or wait.

What we do not deliver — say it early

  • Aryze does not hold the customer's licence — the institution remains the licensed entity
  • Aryze does not become the issuer — the institution issues
  • Aryze does not operate the programme — the institution operates under its own licence
  • No "live in six weeks" — the cycle is 6–18 months; selling speed destroys credibility
  • Careful with "stablecoin" — institutions can dislike the term; use digital cash, tokenised deposits or settlement asset, following the customer's language
  • No "plug-and-play" — institutional infrastructure is not plug-and-play
  • No consortium building, no central-bank functions, no licence delivery
  • Specific networks, jurisdictions and custody partners — confirm per deal with product
