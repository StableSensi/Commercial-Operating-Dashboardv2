Activity sources

Where April activity came from.

Agents, memberships, direct outreach, event conversations and relationship-led introductions. The strongest source quality came from agents and direct relationships when the introduction included buyer context and product fit.

Communication lesson

What language worked.

Pay by Bank was direct to communicate because the buyer pain (chargebacks, scheme costs, settlement speed) is well-defined. Stablecoin Factory and Digital Cash needed private, context-led follow-up rather than open commercial messaging.

What April showed.

Three observations that should change behaviour in May, not generic patterns.

Finding 1

Ownership beats activity.

The strongest April opportunities all had product path, named owner, next step and date. Without those four, leads stalled regardless of how warm the introduction felt.

Finding 2

Product separation is necessary.

Pay by Bank, Stablecoin Factory and Digital Cash produced different signals. Reading them as one pipeline hides where the real movement is.

Finding 3

Stablecoin stage maturity was uneven.

6 of 12 sat at screening, 4 brand new, 1 backlog, 1 proposal. The pool has activity but the screening-to-qualification conversion is the gate that needs work.
