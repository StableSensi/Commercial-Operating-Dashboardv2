Quarterly sales update

Q1 2026 sales update

The pipeline had breadth; the task it set for Q2 was progression, not volume.

Owner: Sales · Written 21 April 2026 · Confidential

At the Q1 readout Aryze held 27 opportunities in the pipeline: 6 Backlog, 7 New, 12 Screening, 1 Proposal and 1 Not interested. The most advanced opportunity was Marton / Stellar — in Proposal, with the case sitting with management on the counterparty side. The strongest commercial case behind it was OpenFX, standing out on fit and timing heading into Q2. In parallel, strategic value was building through a small set of institutional and partner-led tracks: Armenia CBDC, ECB, Banque de France, Banco de España, African Force Group and Maryland.

The quarter's verdict: the pipeline was not yet deep in late-stage conversion, but it was increasingly concentrated around opportunities with real commercial and strategic relevance. The Q2 task set at close: move the right opportunities from Screening into formal decision processes.

The priority opportunities as assessed at the Q1 readout.

Stablecoin Factory · Proposal

Marton / Stellar

The most advanced opportunity on the board and the clearest near-term commercial possibility. The case sat with management on the counterparty side; follow-up had gone out in the readout week, with the last indication that they would revert after Easter.

Next layer

OpenFX

The strongest commercial case in the next layer: relevant, strong product fit, credible underlying need. Earlier dialogue suggested timing was slightly early; Q2 looked materially more promising. Their EMI licence process remained the key dependency.
